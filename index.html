Hello there! Thanks for thinking I'm interesting enough to check up on. If you've come this far, I dare you to say hello before you leave :)

My name is Yixin Zou (pronounced "Yee-Shin Zoh"). I am a PhD candidate at University of Michigan, School of Information (UMSI) advised by Dr. Florian Schaub. I am part of the Security and Privacy Interaction (SPI) Lab and the Michigan Interactive and Social Computing (MISC) group. I also work closely with the Usable Privacy Policy Project team at Carnegie Mellon University, the GWUSEC Lab at George Washington University, and a research team based in New York City that studies computer security and privacy issues for survivors of intimate partner violence (IPV).

My main research focuses on understanding and improving user behaviors in the face of privacy and security threats, e.g., when their personal information is compromised in a data breach, or when they want to exercise privacy choices on websites. I am also involved in research on understanding the privacy risks and expectations of exposure-sensitive populations, such as IPV survivors, older adults, and children.
